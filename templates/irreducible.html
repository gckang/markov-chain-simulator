Irreducible and Aperiodic Markov Chains

Recall, a Markov Chain is irreducible if it consists of only one communication class. If a Markov Chain is both irreducible and aperiodic, the existence of a stationary distribution is guaranteed. In this case, the distribution will satisfy the following. $$\pi P = \pi$$ $$\pi P - \pi = 0$$ $$(P - I)^\intercal\pi^\intercal = 0$$ In the case of an irreducible aperiodic Markov Chain, it suffices to simply find such a nonzero right eigenvector corresponding to an eigenvalue of 1 and then normalizing that eigenvector.
